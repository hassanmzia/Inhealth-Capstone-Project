Priority = (Urgency × 0.6) + (Severity × 0.3) + (Patient_Risk × 0.1)
    Urgency: CRITICAL=1.0, URGENT=0.7, SOON=0.4, ROUTINE=0.1
    Severity: From Agent 18 triage
    Patient_Risk: From Agent 14
```
  - Queue Operations:
    - Insert: O(log n)
    - Extract-min: O(log n) - delivers highest priority notification
    - Peek: O(1) - checks next notification without removing
  
- Batching Rules:
  - CRITICAL: Deliver immediately (no batching)
  - URGENT: Deliver within 5 minutes or batch up to 3 urgent alerts
  - SOON: Batch up to 10 alerts, deliver every 30 minutes
  - ROUTINE: Batch unlimited, deliver once daily at preferred time

- Channel Selection:
  - CRITICAL:
    - Primary: SMS + Page (simultaneous)
    - Secondary: Phone call (if no acknowledgment in 5 minutes)
    - Tertiary: Escalate to backup physician (15 minutes)
  - URGENT:
    - Primary: Email with high-priority flag
    - Secondary: SMS (if no acknowledgment in 30 minutes)
  - SOON:
    - Primary: Email (normal priority)
  - ROUTINE:
    - Primary: EHR inbox message
    - Secondary: Daily digest email
```

```
**Alternative Considered:**

- **Simple Alert Firing**: Causes alert fatigue, 90%+ alerts ignored
- **Rule-Based Without Queue**: Can't prioritize, all alerts treated equally
```

```
### **Agent 20: Patient Notification Agent**
**Primary Algorithm: Multi-Channel Communication Manager + Readability Optimization**

**Algorithm Description:**
Patient-centric notification system:
1. **Channel Preference Engine**: Learns and respects patient communication preferences
2. **Readability Optimizer**: Adjusts language complexity based on health literacy
3. **Timing Optimization**: Delivers messages at times maximizing engagement
4. **Multi-Modal Delivery**: Supports SMS, email, phone calls, app push notifications

**Justification:**
1. **Patient-Centeredness**: Different patients prefer different channels (elderly prefer calls, younger prefer texts). Preference learning maximizes reach.

2. **Health Literacy Adaptation**: 40% of rural adults have limited health literacy. Readability optimization ensures comprehension across education levels.

3. **Engagement Optimization**: Timing matters—messages sent at optimal times have 3× higher open rates. System learns patient-specific optimal times.

4. **Accessibility**: Multi-modal support accommodates visual/hearing impairments, language barriers, limited smartphone access.

5. **Trust Building**: Consistent, clear, respectful communication builds patient trust in automated system.

**Technical Specifications:**
- Channel Preference Learning:
  - **Features**: Age, prior engagement rates by channel, smartphone ownership, vision/hearing status
  - **Algorithm**: Multi-Armed Bandit (Thompson Sampling)
  - **Actions**: SMS, Email, App Push, Phone Call
  - **Reward**: Message opened + action taken (e.g., took medication, reported symptom)
  - **Update**: After each message, update channel success probabilities
  
- Readability Optimization:
  - **Input**: Health literacy score (1-5 scale, from intake assessment)
  - **Method**: Template selection + text simplification
  - **Levels**:
    - Level 1 (Graduate education): Medical terminology, complex sentences
    - Level 2 (College): Moderate terminology, clear explanations
    - Level 3 (High school): Simple language, analogies
    - Level 4 (Middle school): Very simple, visual aids
    - Level 5 (Elementary): Icons, emojis, minimal text
  - **Validation**: Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level, SMOG Index
  
  Example Message Across Literacy Levels:
```
  Level 1: "Your HbA1c is 8.2%, indicating suboptimal glycemic control. Consider intensification of antihyperglycemic therapy."
  
  Level 3: "Your diabetes blood test (HbA1c) is 8.2%, which is higher than the goal of 7.0%. This means your blood sugar has been too high. We should talk about adjusting your medications."
  
  Level 5: "😊 Blood test result: Your diabetes number is 8.2. 🎯 Goal is 7 or less. 💊 Let's work together to improve this!"
```

- Timing Optimization:
  - **Data**: Historical open times, action times, patient-reported preferences
  - **Algorithm**: Gaussian Mixture Model clustering patient behavior
  - **Output**: Optimal send window for each patient (e.g., "7-9 AM" or "6-8 PM")
  - **Constraints**: Never send 10 PM - 7 AM (unless CRITICAL alert)
  
- Content Types:
  1. **Medication Reminders**: Daily reminders with dosing instructions
  2. **Appointment Reminders**: 48 hours, 24 hours, 2 hours before
  3. **Lab Result Notifications**: Results with interpretation
  4. **Health Coaching**: From Agent 16, personalized tips
  5. **Symptom Check-ins**: Weekly surveys (How are you feeling?)
  6. **Educational Content**: Disease management, lifestyle tips
  7. **Alerts**: Urgent actions needed (Schedule appointment, Call doctor)
  
- Multi-Language Support:
  - Primary languages: English, Spanish
  - Machine translation for other languages
  - Cultural adaptation (not just word-for-word translation)
  
- Accessibility Features:
  - **Vision Impairment**: Large font SMS, text-to-speech phone calls, high-contrast emails
  - **Hearing Impairment**: Text-based channels only, video calls with captions
  - **Limited Smartphone**: SMS-only mode, phone calls
  - **No Internet**: SMS and phone calls only
```
