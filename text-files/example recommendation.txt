### **Agent 18: Priority & Triage Agent**
**Primary Algorithm: Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) with Fuzzy Logic**

**Algorithm Description:**
Decision framework evaluating multiple criteria:
1. **Criteria**: Clinical severity, time sensitivity, resource availability, patient preference
2. **Fuzzy Logic**: Handles uncertainty in clinical judgments (e.g., "moderately severe" pneumonia)
3. **Weighted Scoring**: Combines criteria into overall priority score
4. **Routing Rules**: Maps priority scores to action pathways

**Justification:**
1. **Multi-Dimensional Decisions**: Triage requires balancing competing factors (severity vs. available resources vs. patient preference). MCDA explicitly models these trade-offs.

2. **Uncertainty Handling**: Clinical presentations have inherent uncertainty. Fuzzy logic represents "borderline severe" better than binary classification.

3. **Interpretability**: Weighted scores show clinician exactly why decision was made: "Priority: HIGH due to severe finding (weight 0.4 × severity 0.9) + high clinical risk (weight 0.3 × risk 0.8)."

4. **Flexible Thresholds**: Weights and thresholds can be adjusted based on system capacity (e.g., lower threshold for hospitalization when beds available).

5. **Consistency**: Algorithmic triage ensures consistent prioritization, reducing human cognitive biases.

**Technical Specifications:**
- Decision Criteria (7):
  1. **Clinical Severity** (weight 0.30):
     - Life-threatening (1.0): STEMI, stroke, severe hypoglycemia
     - Severe (0.75): Pneumonia with hypoxia, uncontrolled AFib
     - Moderate (0.50): Elevated BP, poor glucose control
     - Mild (0.25): Routine follow-up needs
  
  2. **Time Sensitivity** (weight 0.25):
     - Immediate (<1 hour): Cardiac arrest, severe hypoglycemia
     - Urgent (<4 hours): STEMI, stroke, severe sepsis
     - Soon (<24 hours): Pneumonia, new AFib
     - Routine (<7 days): Medication adjustment
  
  3. **Risk Score from Agent 14** (weight 0.20):
     - Critical (76-100): 1.0
     - High (51-75): 0.75
     - Moderate (26-50): 0.50
     - Low (0-25): 0.25
  
  4. **Trajectory** (weight 0.10):
     - Rapidly worsening: 1.0
     - Slowly worsening: 0.75
     - Stable: 0.50
     - Improving: 0.25
  
  5. **Comorbidity Burden** (weight 0.08):
     - Based on number of active conditions, medication count
  
  6. **Social Factors** (weight 0.05):
     - Lives alone, limited support: Higher priority
     - Strong support system: Can manage at home
  
  7. **Patient Preference** (weight 0.02):
     - Respects advance directives, treatment preferences

- Fuzzy Logic Membership Functions:
  - Example: "Severe" pneumonia
    - Oxygen saturation 85%: Membership = 1.0 (definitely severe)
    - Oxygen saturation 90%: Membership = 0.6 (moderately severe)
    - Oxygen saturation 94%: Membership = 0.2 (borderline severe)
    - Oxygen saturation 97%: Membership = 0.0 (not severe)

- Priority Score Calculation:
```
  Priority_Score = Σ (weight_i × fuzzy_value_i)
  
  Example:
  - Severity (0.30 × 0.9) = 0.27
  - Time Sensitivity (0.25 × 0.8) = 0.20
  - Risk Score (0.20 × 0.85) = 0.17
  - Trajectory (0.10 × 0.7) = 0.07
  - Comorbidity (0.08 × 0.6) = 0.05
  - Social (0.05 × 0.5) = 0.03
  - Preference (0.02 × 0.5) = 0.01
  
  Total Priority Score = 0.80 (HIGH)
```
